Macro Strategist · TPTraders Desk · Monday, July 13, 2026
Strait of Hormuz Re-escalation Collides with CPI Week — The Market Has No Easy Exit.

Monday's session opens with a structurally uncomfortable cross-asset configuration: every major risk indicator is flashing caution simultaneously, yet the severity does not yet rise to crisis-level. ES at 7590.50 (-0.39%) and NQ at 29694.75 (-1.12%) are under pressure, but not in freefall. The VIX at 16.43 (+9.31%) is elevated but well below the 20-25 zone that historically marks genuine regime fear. The real problem is the narrative context: over the weekend, fresh US-Iran strikes near the Strait of Hormuz — with Iran claiming the strait is closed 'until further notice' and US Central Command denying it — have sent WTI crude surging +3.77% to $74.10. That single move is sufficient to meaningfully complicate Tuesday's CPI print and Chair Warsh's first congressional testimony, both of which were already on a knife's edge.

The cross-asset correlations on this tape are telling a stagflationary story. The 10Y yield rising to 4.59% while gold falls -1.09% to $4,068.70 means real yields are the primary driver — investors are not fleeing to traditional safe havens but are instead selling duration-sensitive assets across the board. The 2Y yield collapsing -1.82% to 3.89% simultaneously suggests the short end is pricing some probability of a policy error from a hawkish Fed that overtightens into a geopolitical supply shock. The 10Y-2Y spread at +70 bps and steepening rapidly is the clearest bear-steepener the market has seen in this cycle, and it is textbook stagflation signaling. Bitcoin at $62,542 (-1.89%) confirms crypto is trading in lockstep with risk assets, providing no diversification benefit today. Copper's +0.30% nudge is the one dissenting voice, suggesting China demand has not rolled over — yet.

What would change the picture intraday: a credible statement from US Central Command confirming Hormuz passage remains open and tanker traffic is normalizing would immediately deflate WTI and the associated inflation-risk premium, potentially recovering ES back toward the 7620-7640 range. Conversely, a major bank earnings beat from one of the big reporters this week could provide a fundamental floor, particularly for the Dow which has the most direct financial sector exposure. A DXY break above 102 would be the FX confirmation that risk-off is deepening; stability near 100.97 keeps the picture 'mixed' rather than 'crisis.'

The clearest risk to the bullish thesis is a hot CPI print Tuesday morning arriving on top of today's oil shock. If June CPI comes in above consensus with core proving sticky — a meaningful probability given shelter and medical care components — Warsh's testimony will harden the Fed's hawkish posture just as the geopolitical risk premium is already driving energy prices higher. That combination: rising 10Y yields above 4.65%, persistent VIX above 18, NQ breaking 29500, and WTI sustaining above $76, would mark a full regime shift from the 'earnings-led bull market' narrative that has defined the first half of 2026 and would require a fundamental reassessment of equity risk premiums across the board.
