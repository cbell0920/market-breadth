Macro Strategist · TPTraders Desk · Friday, July 17, 2026
AI Fatigue Meets Hormuz Heat: A Week-End Reckoning for Expensive Themes

Friday's session is closing a turbulent week with two macro forces in direct collision. On one side, the AI-valuation unwind that has been quietly building since mid-week crystallized on Thursday when Taiwan Semiconductor reported a 77% annual earnings gain only to see its shares fall more than 4% — a classic 'buy the rumor, sell the news' reaction that has now metastasized into a broader Nasdaq de-rating. Netflix's post-close miss amplified that narrative, shifting the earnings-season framing from 'beats are enough' to 'are multiples defensible at all?' On the other side, US-Iran hostilities have widened materially, with US strikes targeting infrastructure in Iran's Hormozgan province and fresh UKMTO reports of tanker incidents in the Gulf of Oman — developments keeping crude firmly bid even as equities crack.

The IOF confluence data captures this split-register market precisely. MNQ shows mixed EMA alignment with a bearish weekly structure — the Nasdaq's trend deterioration is not merely intraday noise but is confirmed across both the daily EMA stack and the sequential weekly candle relationship. M2K shares the same bearish structure, consistent with small-cap underperformance in a higher-for-longer rate regime where refinancing risk is most acute. HG (Copper) mirrors this — mixed EMA alignment paired with a bearish weekly structure — and its -1.89% spot decline today reinforces the global growth-demand doubt that runs beneath the surface of the oil-driven headline volatility.

Not all confluences are leaning the same direction, and that asymmetry matters. MES and MYM both carry mixed EMA alignment but retain bullish weekly structures, meaning the S&P 500 and Dow have not yet broken their sequential higher-high / higher-low pattern on the weekly timeframe — a meaningful distinction from the Nasdaq's confirmed bearish structure. CL (WTI Oil) sits in the same mixed EMA / bullish structure camp, which aligns cleanly with today's +2.06% spot move and the geopolitical risk premium embedded in crude. MGC (Micro Gold) presents an interesting tension: bearish EMA alignment on the daily bars but a bullish weekly structure — the metal is holding its longer-horizon uptrend even as the daily trend has softened, consistent with gold hovering near $3,995 without breaking decisively in either direction as dollar and yield forces offset each other. SIL (Micro Silver) shares the same bearish daily EMA / bullish weekly structure profile, though silver's -1.01% session decline underlines that industrial-demand concerns are currently winning the near-term argument.

The macro backdrop anchoring all of this is the Fed. Fed Vice Chair Jefferson flagged openness to a rate hike later in 2026 if inflation fails to cool, and the University of Michigan consumer sentiment release due later today will test whether households are beginning to feel the weight of elevated borrowing costs. The 2Y yield's sharp -0.65% drop today tells a story of front-end rate-cut pricing creeping back in — yet the 10Y simultaneously rises to 4.57%, a curve steepening dynamic that typically reflects a market pricing 'risk-off for growth, but inflation stays sticky' — precisely the stagflationary middle-ground that is most uncomfortable for equity multiples. With the 10Y Treasury showing both bullish EMA alignment and bullish weekly structure in the IOF data, the bond market's own trend structure is reinforcing the idea that long-end yields have further to run, and that the discount-rate headwind for equities is not yet resolved heading into the weekend.
