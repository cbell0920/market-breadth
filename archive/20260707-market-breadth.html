Macro Strategist · TPTraders Desk · Tuesday, July 7, 2026
The Dow hits records while the Nasdaq bleeds chips — Tuesday is not a market story, it's a rotation story.

The macro verdict for Tuesday, July 7 is unambiguously Mixed, and the divergence between YM (+0.31%, fresh ATH above 53,540) and NQ (-0.79%, 29,704) is the session's defining signal. This is not broad risk-off — it is surgical sector rotation amplified by two structural events colliding simultaneously: the Asian semiconductor selloff (Kospi circuit breaker triggered, Samsung and SK Hynix each down 9%+) and SpaceX's (SPCX) Nasdaq-100 inclusion forcing an estimated $4.3–$10 billion in passive buying into a 3–5% float, distorting QQQ price action until at least 11:00 AM ET. The fundamental backdrop supporting rotation INTO cyclicals, financials, and small caps is robust: Friday's NFP at 57K vs. 115K est. pushed September rate-hike probability below 51%, the 2Y yield collapsed 26bps to 3.89%, and the 2s10s curve steepened to +59bps — all textbook inputs for a small-cap (RTY +0.48% at 3,041) and financial-sector bid.

Cross-asset correlations reinforce the mixed verdict rather than resolving it. Gold at $4,178.80 (+0.27%) and oil at $69.01 (+0.67%) are both edging higher on Strait of Hormuz geopolitical risk — a Qatari LNG carrier was struck near Oman on Monday, testing the US-Iran ceasefire — but neither move is large enough to constitute a systemic safe-haven flight. The DXY at 100.94 (+0.08%) is a non-factor, pinned in a range. Copper at $6.23 (-0.05%) is refusing to confirm a growth acceleration narrative, while silver's underperformance (-0.60% to $61.95) confirms the gold bid is defensive rather than industrial. BTC at $63,587 (-0.64%), pulling back from its $64,500 two-week high amid falling open interest and weak ETF inflows, is echoing the cautious tilt.

What would change the picture intraday: (1) A hot read on the US May Trade Balance or NY Fed 1-Year Inflation Expectations could reverse the dovish NFP tailwind and spike the 2Y back above 4.00%, blowing out the rationale for RTY and financials outperformance and potentially dragging ES back below 7,537. (2) A SPCX stabilization post-11AM that allows NQ to recover toward the 30,000 level would restore a more neutral cross-index picture and could spark a tech short squeeze. (3) Any escalation of Strait of Hormuz attacks that pushes WTI above $70-72 could flip energy's contribution from bullish noise to macro headwind, reigniting inflation fears precisely when the market has priced in soft-landing comfort.

The clearest risk to the rotation thesis is the VIX's quiet +1.80% rise to 15.85 combined with SKEW at an elevated 145.38. While the VIX term structure remains in healthy contango (spot 15.85 → front-month 17.35 → VIX3M 18.78), the elevated SKEW signals that sophisticated institutional players are buying index downside protection even as equity put/call ratios (~0.53) look complacently bullish. This divergence — retail and equity options calm, index tail hedgers active — is the hallmark of a market that has climbed far and fast (Dow ATH, S&P near ATH at 7,584) and where a single catalyst, namely Wednesday's FOMC minutes, could rapidly collapse the contango and flip the regime. Trade the rotation, respect the skew, and treat any NQ recovery toward 30,000 before the Fed minutes as a risk-reward opportunity to trim, not add.
