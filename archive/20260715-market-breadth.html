With spot VIX at 16.34 — well below its long-run mean of ~19-20 — the VIX futures curve is firmly in contango, the normal state that prevails roughly 80% of trading days; longer-dated contracts are priced above spot, reflecting structural mean-reversion premium rather than near-term fear. The steep contango imposes negative roll yield on long-vol products like VXX and UVXY, systematically rewarding short-volatility strategies in the current calm-ish regime.
The 2Y-10Y spread has widened sharply to +69bps on today's session, with the 2-year collapsing 202bps (in price-to-yield terms, -2.02%) as markets aggressively price out near-term Fed hike probability following the soft CPI. The curve is bull-steepening — a dynamic where short rates fall faster than long rates — the classic early-cycle pivot signal, though the 10Y holding at 4.58% reflects lingering inflation risk-premium tied to Hormuz/oil rather than a pure growth scare.

Macro Strategist · TPTraders Desk · Wednesday, July 15, 2026
CPI bought the dip, but Hormuz is the unknown variable that could unwind the whole trade before the close.

Wednesday's session opens with a structurally Mixed verdict that leans cautiously constructive. The architecture of the move is built on Tuesday's CPI surprise — headline inflation declining sharply and core CPI at 2.6% annually vs. the 2.9% consensus — which triggered an aggressive 2-year Treasury rally (-2.02% today to 3.89%) that removes near-term Fed hike probability and revives the AI/growth equity bid. NQ at 29,921.75 (+0.44%) is doing the session's heavy lifting while ES grinds at 7,600.75 (+0.13%) and the Dow is effectively unchanged at 52,795. The divergence between NQ and RTY (-0.04% at 2,978.60) is the critical tell: if this were a genuinely clean risk-on tape, small caps would be outrunning mega-cap tech on rate-cut pricing. Their underperformance suggests either credit stress concerns or energy-cost pass-through fears specific to domestically-oriented businesses. The flat copper at $6.374 and modestly bid oil at $79.52 reinforce this interpretation — the global growth signal is absent.

The cross-asset correlations worth tracking today are threefold. First, the gold-vs-equity relationship: gold at $4,045.40 (-0.60%) falling while equities rise is the classic risk-on fingerprint and is the session's clearest confirmation that today is a relief rally, not a fear-driven flight. Second, the DXY-NQ inverse: the dollar at 100.98 is essentially flat, a benign backdrop for tech multiples; any DXY spike above 101.50 would compress NQ growth valuations and signal the Hormuz safety bid is overwhelming the CPI dove trade. Third, the 2Y yield vs. RTY: small caps should be the primary beneficiary of a 2-year collapsing to 3.89%, yet RTY is flat — if this gap doesn't close by midday, it is a breadth warning that the rally is narrowing to mega-cap tech only.

Intraday, the picture could change materially on two catalysts. The June PPI release (8:30 ET) is the most immediate binary risk — a hot print would force the market to re-examine yesterday's CPI relief trade, spike the 2Y back toward 4.10%, and hit tech multiples. Fed Chair Warsh's congressional testimony is the second catalyst: he has already telegraphed 'no tolerance for persistently elevated inflation,' and any hawkish pivot on rate guidance — particularly framed around Hormuz-driven oil price risk — could rapidly reprice the session. Watch also for any FOMC-commentary around the PPI report from NY Fed President Williams, also speaking today.

The clearest risk to the session thesis is the Strait of Hormuz situation. A U.S. military maritime blockade on Iranian ports was reportedly activated at 04:00 ET this morning, and a new round of U.S. strikes against Iran is confirmed. Oil at $79.52 reflects a partial risk premium but not a supply-shock price — if Brent breaks back above $85-87 (Brent's recent spike high cited in reporting), inflation re-escalation fears return in force, the 2Y retraces, and the entire CPI relief trade unwinds. The index put/call at 1.01 tells you institutional desks are hedging exactly this scenario. The contrarian read from the total put/call at 0.86 (9-day avg 0.92, 82nd percentile) is that fear is elevated enough to be a floor under equities — but Hormuz is the black-swan variable that could override the sentiment indicator entirely. Trade the levels: ES above 7,550 and NQ above 29,700 maintain the bullish structure; a close below those levels with VIX back above 17.50 flips the session verdict to Risk-Off.
