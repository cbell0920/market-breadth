Macro Strategist · TPTraders Desk · Thursday, July 9, 2026
Iran strikes, steepening curves, and a copper rip — the market refuses to pick a lane.

Thursday's session is defined by a structural tension between two powerful narratives competing for price discovery in real time. The U.S. conducted a second consecutive day of airstrikes on Iran, which responded by targeting Gulf states — an escalation that would historically trigger a full-blown risk-off cascade. Instead, ES futures are holding 7543.75 (+0.20%), NQ is outperforming at 29699.75 (+0.78%), and copper is screaming +2.13% to $6.24/lb. The market's refusal to sell is itself a signal: the Iran risk is being treated as bounded and partially hedged (equity PCR was 1.33 as of July 3), and the AI/semiconductor earnings growth story is powerful enough to absorb geopolitical noise that would have been devastating in a prior macro regime.

The most important cross-asset signal this session is the bear-steepening in Treasuries: the 10Y has surged to 4.57% (+0.88%) while the 2Y has rallied to 3.89% (-0.89%), widening the 2s10s spread by roughly 17bps on the day. This is not a benign steepening driven by growth optimism — it is bear-steepening driven by the Fed's own minutes, which flagged that 'a few policymakers' saw grounds for a rate hike, pushing September hike probability to ~70%. Gold at $4,115.20 is simultaneously pricing in the geopolitical tail AND the inflation re-acceleration tail. The DXY's flat print at 101.00 is the circuit breaker preventing a full stagflation trade from erupting: if the dollar breaks above 102.50, the playbook shifts from 'Mixed' to 'Risk-Off' almost instantly.

The VIX at 17.00 (+0.59%) remains in contango — a structural signal that options markets are not pricing systemic crisis, only elevated session-by-session uncertainty. With the VX6/VX1 slope compressed well below historical norms, the vol term structure is offering cheap tail protection, making it an asymmetrically attractive hedge for longs into next week's CPI (July 14) and financial earnings season (JPM, BAC, GS, WFC, C all reporting). PepsiCo's earnings beat and full-year guidance hold provide a stabilizing consumer staples datapoint, and SK Hynix's 7x oversubscribed ADR debut injects structural semis demand into the NQ narrative heading into the cash open.

The clearest risk to the current 'Mixed/Constructive' thesis is a WTI break above $76 — at that level, energy-inflation fears would re-price Fed expectations even further hawkishly, punishing the rate-sensitive components of YM and RTY (currently lagging at 2977.80, below the key 3000 psychological level) and forcing a reassessment of NQ multiples above 29700. Watch the 10Y at 4.57% as the fulcrum level: a sustained move toward 4.65-4.70% would be the signal that the yield market is breaking consensus equity assumptions, and would convert this 'Mixed' tape into a clean 'Risk-Off' regime before the close.
