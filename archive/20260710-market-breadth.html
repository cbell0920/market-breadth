Macro Strategist · TPTraders Desk · Friday, July 10, 2026
Friday's tape is a tug-of-war between AI momentum and a geopolitical premium that refuses to fully clear — the bond market is making the clearest call.

The session tone on Friday, July 10 is best described as Mixed with a slight risk-on lean, driven by a diverging futures complex. The bond market is sending the most constructive signal: the 10Y Treasury at 4.54% (-0.66%) and the 2Y at 3.89% (-1.35%) represent a meaningful bull steepener that directly reduces the discount rate burden on long-duration growth assets. That yield tailwind is what's keeping ES futures at 7585.50 essentially flat — rather than negative — even as NQ at 29841.75 digests Thursday's aggressive semiconductor rally. The Dow's +0.14% bid at 52836 confirms that rotation, not distribution, is the dominant intraday force.

Cross-asset correlations are telling a nuanced story. Gold's -0.61% retreat to $4115.60 alongside WTI's +0.62% rise to $72.53 suggests markets are pricing Strait of Hormuz tension as an oil supply risk — not a full-spectrum flight-to-safety event. That interpretation is supported by VIX at 15.93 in a compressed contango (VX6/VX1 ~1.062), well below the ~19–20 long-run mean, with no sign of backwardation that would signal acute stress. Bitcoin's +1.90% push to $64,387 — outperforming every other asset class on the session — is the speculative risk appetite barometer, and it is green. The CBOE Equity Put/Call at ~0.69 confirms calls are dominating flow, consistent with the AI/semiconductor positioning that has dominated 2026's second half.

What would change the picture intraday: (1) A geopolitical escalation headline from the Strait of Hormuz — specifically any reported closure or tanker seizure — would spike WTI above $75, drive a VIX jump above 17.50, and force ES below the 7543 Thursday close support. (2) Dallas Fed President Logan (2026 FOMC voter) is speaking today — any hawkish deviation from the Warsh data-dependence framework, particularly if framed around the 4.2% CPI print, could re-steepen the front end of the yield curve and pressure NQ back toward the 29700 session support. (3) SK Hynix's Nasdaq debut is the wildcard for semiconductor sentiment — a 'buy-the-news' reversal in SKHY could drag the SOX and pull NQ futures further negative.

The clearest risk to the bullish lean is the macro calendar ahead: next Tuesday's CPI (July 14) arrives alongside Congressional testimony from Fed Chair Kevin Warsh and the full bank earnings barrage (JPM, BAC, GS, WFC, C). If CPI comes in above the 4.2% May print, the 10Y yield's current 4.54% level — itself a compression from the 4.58% auction level earlier this week — could rapidly re-rate toward 4.70%+, collapsing the yield tailwind that is single-handedly keeping ES futures from rolling over. Into the weekend, the path of least resistance is a quiet, low-volume grind higher in value names (YM) and a modest tech giveback (NQ), with RTY's 3008.50 acting as the key breadth barometer: a close above 3020 would be the cleanest confirmation that Friday's mixed tone resolves to the upside.
