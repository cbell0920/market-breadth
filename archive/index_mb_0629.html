Week in Context

The final week of Q2 2026 opens with a genuine rotation story. Technology sold off sharply last week — semiconductor index -7.9%, QQQ -5.7% from its ATH — despite Micron posting the most important earnings beat of the year. The divergence between strong AI earnings and falling tech stock prices reflects concerns about spiraling AI costs, the reported OpenAI IPO delay, and Apple/Microsoft announcing consumer price increases. These are sentiment headwinds, not fundamental deterioration.

But the underlying breadth picture was constructive throughout. Advancing shares outnumbered declining shares every day even as major indexes fell. The S&P Equal Weight index rose 0.7% Thursday when the S&P 500 was flat. Healthcare led with XLV +3.03%. Industrials and financials participated. Money rotated — it did not leave. That is a healthy bull market in the middle of a sector rotation, not the beginning of a bear market.

This week Iran peace talk progress is adding risk-on sentiment. US officials confirmed talks remain on track. Comcast announced a major spinoff pre-market — NBCUniversal and Sky becoming separate publicly traded companies. SpaceX was announced for Nasdaq 100 inclusion Friday. These are all structural positives for market confidence. NFP Friday is the week's defining event.

Breadth Assessment

The breadth picture is one of the most important signals this week. When major indexes fall but advance/decline is positive and equal-weight outperforms, it tells you the market is redistributing — not liquidating. Institutional money is moving from expensive, crowded positions (mega-cap tech) into value, defensives, and cyclicals (healthcare, industrials, financials). This is classic late-stage bull market sector rotation.

The risk scenario is if NFP on Friday is so strong that it forces aggressive September hike pricing (above 80–90%), which could compress multiples broadly and damage even the defensive rotation. At 68% currently priced, there is room for the rotation to continue. Watch the 10-year yield — if it breaks above 4.5%, the rate headwind becomes too large for any sector to ignore.

NFP Preview — The Week's Pivot Point

Non-Farm Payrolls on Friday July 2 is the single most important data event of the week. The September rate hike is currently 68% priced. A strong NFP (above 180K, unemployment holding below 4%) pushes that to 80–90% and extends USD strength, pressures tech multiples, and benefits financials. A weak NFP (below 120K, unemployment rising) drops the probability to 40–50% and gives tech stocks a meaningful relief rally.

The week's setup: today is light (no data), Tuesday is light, Wednesday brings ADP (preview) and ISM Manufacturing, Thursday is a US holiday (Independence Day) creating thin liquidity conditions, Friday brings NFP. Build positions today and Tuesday in the direction you want to hold into Friday. The holiday Thursday is the danger — thin volume amplifies any early NFP reaction Friday morning.

Senior Analyst Note
TPTraders Senior Market Analyst — June 29, 2026

Q2 is ending with a rotation story that matters. The final week of the quarter opens with tech under pressure, healthcare leading, and the Comcast spinoff announcement reshaping the media landscape. The breadth picture is the most important signal — advancing outnumbered declining shares consistently last week even as the Nasdaq fell 5.7% from its ATH. Institutions are repositioning within equities, not leaving them. That is a fundamentally different market than the one that exists when declining shares dominate.

This week is about NFP on Friday. Everything between today and Thursday July 4 (US holiday) is positioning for that data point. The ADP report Wednesday gives a preview. The ISM Manufacturing data Wednesday gives an industrial health read. Consumer confidence and JOLTS Tuesday give a consumer health read. If all of these come in strong, by Thursday the September hike is near-certain and you want financials, healthcare, and short USD pairs going into Friday. If any of them disappoint materially, trim risk before Friday NFP.

The rotation out of semis deserves careful watch. The semiconductor selloff last week (-7.9% for the PHLX chip index) happened DESPITE Micron posting the most important earnings beat of the year. That tells you the selling was sentiment-driven — AI cost concerns and crowded positioning, not fundamental deterioration. Tom Lee of Fundstrat called it "a classic opportunity to buy the dip in chip stocks." SpaceX's Nasdaq 100 inclusion creates structural forced buying for QQQ. The question is timing — semis could fall further before institutional buyers step in. Don't chase the dip prematurely. Wait for a confirmed base candle at $700 QQQ support before re-entering tech.

Iran peace talk progress is the wild card. If talks accelerate toward a formal agreement this week, oil could fall below $65/bbl — amplifying the disinflationary impulse from the Hormuz reopening. That would be bullish for equities broadly (lower inflation = less hawkish Fed) but bearish for gold and energy. Watch oil closely — it is the transmission mechanism from geopolitics to markets this week.
