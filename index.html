Macro Environment

Tuesday's session delivered a clear message: the US equity market is dangerously dependent on semiconductor leadership, and that dependency is now a liability. South Korea's KOSPI plummeted 9.99% — its steepest drop in over three months — as overseas investors dumped Samsung and SK Hynix on concerns that the chip sector's rally had become overheated. US chip stocks fell in sympathy, dragging the Nasdaq -2.21% and the S&P 500 -1.44%. The critical detail, however, is that approximately 60% of S&P 500 stocks — roughly 299 of 500 — actually advanced on the day. Staples (+1.79%), health care (+1.29%), real estate (+1.35%), utilities (+0.55%), and energy (+0.57%) were all positive. The index fell because technology (-3.49% / -4.13% for the S&P tech sector) is the single largest weight and it sold off hard.

The rate environment is the structural headwind that won't go away. The 10-year Treasury yield sits at 4.499% — its highest in about two weeks — and the 2-year is at 4.205%, the highest since February 2025. Both Deutsche Bank and BofA Global Research have revised their forecasts to include a September rate hike following the Fed's hawkish tone under Chair Warsh. Markets are now pricing approximately 68% probability of a September hike (up from 29% before the June 17 FOMC). This is particularly problematic for high-multiple tech and growth names, whose valuations are extremely sensitive to the discount rate.

A Bank of America research note warning of up to three rate hikes this year is being cited as an additional dampener on sentiment. While oil prices are softening on Iran deal progress — a disinflationary signal — the 2Y yield at its highest since February 2025 tells you the bond market is not buying the disinflation story yet. The PCE report today is the single most critical data point: a hot print entrenches the hike narrative and accelerates the rotation away from growth into defensives and financials. A miss could provide temporary relief to tech, but the structural repricing has likely begun.

The broader picture: defensives are winning, AI-infrastructure names within tech are the last men standing, and the market is making an orderly (for now) adjustment to a higher-for-longer rate environment. The VIX at 19.49 — up 12.8% on the day but still well below the March peak of 31.05 — signals elevated concern without crisis-level fear. Today's market will be watching Micron earnings after the bell as a tell on whether the semi selloff is sentiment-driven or fundamental.

Senior Analyst Note
TPTraders Senior Equities Analyst — June 24, 2026

Yesterday's selloff is being misread by those calling it a broad market breakdown. It isn't — yet. Approximately 60% of S&P 500 stocks advanced on Tuesday even as the index fell 1.44%. The damage was concentrated in the technology sector, which dragged the headline number. The real story is a rotational shift that has been building for weeks: money is leaving hyper-growth tech and moving into defensives, financials, and select utilities. That rotation is rational given the rate environment. The question is whether this is a healthy pause or the beginning of a more serious tech unwind.

Micron earnings tonight are the near-term tell. The KOSPI rout was triggered by concerns that the semiconductor rally had become detached from fundamentals. If Micron reports strong demand guidance for DRAM and NAND — driven by AI infrastructure buildout — the selloff in US chips looks like an overreaction to Korean-market dynamics and the tech sector bounces tomorrow. If Micron is cautious or guides down, the thesis that semis are topping becomes much harder to dismiss. Position accordingly before the bell: either reduce tech exposure and wait, or hold with defined stop-loss levels.

The rate picture is the structural overlay that doesn't go away after tonight. The 10-year at 4.5% and 2-year at 4.2%, with 68% probability of a September hike, is a genuinely hostile environment for high-multiple growth stocks. QQQ is up 32% over the past year — that multiple expansion was built on hopes of rate cuts that are now off the table. Reversion to mean in an environment where risk-free rates are at 4.2% is a real scenario, not a tail risk. The technically important levels to watch on QQQ are $701 on the downside and $725 on the upside. A close below $701 on volume would signal a more serious breakdown toward $686.

The playbook for today: PCE at 08:30 ET sets the tone. Hot (≥+0.4% core m/m): defensives continue working, reduce tech, favour financials on steeper curve, VIX likely tests 22. Soft miss (<+0.2% core): relief rally in tech, buy the QQQ dip near $701–$713, but size carefully — the structural repricing isn't over on one data point. Micron after bell then resets the semiconductor narrative for the next two weeks. Don't be fully positioned before you have both data points.
