Rates Implications for Equities

The yield curve is the dominant macro variable for equities right now. The 10-year at 4.408% and rising is a meaningful headwind for high-multiple growth names. The relationship is direct: higher rates = lower present value of future earnings = compression of P/E multiples. This is why Nasdaq has pulled back ~5% from its June 2 ATH even as earnings remain strong.

The positive side of higher rates: Financials benefit from wider net interest margins. Banks can earn more on loans vs deposits. The Fed stress test results released this week confirmed major US banks can handle a severe economic downturn — removing a tail risk. This supports XLF rotation.

PCE data at 8:30 today will determine whether the 10-year yield pushes toward 4.5%+ (hot print) or pulls back toward 4.3% (soft miss). The equity market implications are significant — each 25bp move in the 10-year has roughly a 2–3% inverse impact on Nasdaq valuations.

Senior Analyst Note
TPTraders Senior Market Analyst — June 25, 2026

The market is having a conversation about two incompatible things simultaneously. Micron's earnings confirm that the AI capex cycle is intact and accelerating — Big Tech has signaled AI capital expenditure rising past $700 billion in 2026 from $400 billion in 2025, and Micron's results validate that spend. This is unambiguously bullish for semiconductors. At the same time, the Fed under Kevin Warsh is making clear that rates will stay higher for longer, and possibly go higher still. Those two things — strong AI earnings growth and rising rates — create a tug-of-war that determines the market's direction from here.

PCE data at 8:30 is the referee. A soft PCE (below +0.4% headline) gives the AI bull thesis room to run — lower rates reduce the discount on future earnings and allow growth multiples to expand. A hot print (+0.5% or above) forces the market to price a near-certain September hike and potentially October too, which compresses multiples even as earnings grow. The setup is not binary — markets can hold both truths — but the path of least resistance changes depending on the print.

Breadth is the canary. Today's pre-market Micron surge is real, but if it stays concentrated in semiconductors and the Dow, Russell 2000, and healthcare don't participate, the rally is thin. Genuine bull markets need broad participation. The fact that small caps lagged badly on Wednesday (-0.96%) while the Dow led (+0.35%) suggests institutional rotation into quality over speculation. That's not bearish — it's mature bull market behavior — but it means individual stock selection matters more than just being long the index.

Actionable today: Don't chase the Micron gap at the open unless PCE comes in soft or in line. If PCE is hot, wait 30–45 minutes for the initial volatility to settle before taking any new positions. The best setup on a hot PCE is shorting gold via GLD puts — the macro setup is clear, the technical setup is clean (below $4,000), and the risk management is straightforward. On a soft PCE, the Nasdaq rally broadens and QQQ calls become compelling above $718.
