Macro Context — H2 2026 Opens

The second half of 2026 opens with a significantly different macro backdrop than Q2 started with. Q2 began with fears about the Middle East conflict and ended with a tentative truce and historic equity gains. H2 begins with that truce under strain (Iran rejected Doha), markets digesting the best quarter in years, and a compressed data calendar — ADP + ISM + Warsh today, NFP tomorrow, markets closed Friday. The macro narrative is shifting from "can we avoid a rate hike?" to "when is the September hike?"

The JOLTS report yesterday was arguably more important than any single data point this week. Job openings climbed to a two-year high — this directly undermines any dovish Fed narrative. A tight labour market means wage pressure continues, which means services inflation stays elevated, which means the Fed cannot cut and is more likely to hike. Markets have repriced accordingly — September hike probability is now approaching 70%. Warsh's speech at the ECB Forum in Sintra today (9:30 AM ET) will either confirm or challenge this pricing.

The commodity complex tells a different story than equities. While stocks gained 14-20% in Q2, oil fell 30% and gold fell 14% — both at or near their worst quarterly performances in over a decade. The oil decline is supply-driven (Iran exports recovering post-Hormuz + Russian exports at record levels) and is structurally disinflationary for the US economy. This is a two-edged sword: lower oil reduces headline PCE (potentially easing the Fed's inflation burden) but the services inflation component (wages, shelter) remains sticky regardless of oil prices.

Bitcoin's muted response to today's risk-off is worth noting. BTC is essentially flat (+0.08% to $58,346) in a session where equities futures are down 0.3-0.5%. This divergence from prior risk-off episodes — where crypto typically sold off aggressively alongside equities — suggests the broader crypto market is at a point where positioning is "washed out" per multiple analysts. Limited forced liquidation risk at current levels. Bitcoin remains a risk-on/risk-off indicator but its signal is currently ambiguous.

Senior Analyst Note
TPTraders Senior Market Analyst — July 1, 2026

H2 2026 opens exactly as it should after a historic quarter — with discipline, not euphoria. The S&P 500 gained 14% in Q2, the Nasdaq gained 20%, and both sectors pulled back to open July 1. This is not a warning sign. This is healthy. Historically, markets that post large quarterly gains tend to consolidate in the early weeks of the following quarter before resuming the trend or establishing a new one. The Iran news and the data calendar are creating the mechanical excuse for that consolidation — but the underlying driver is normal institutional rebalancing after exceptional gains.

The most important thing happening today is Warsh at Sintra at 9:30 AM ET. The JOLTS data yesterday gave him a hawkish backdrop: job openings at a two-year high means the labour market is tighter than expected. If Warsh explicitly signals that September is on the table — or that the Fed is evaluating reducing its balance sheet — the rate narrative shifts from "possible hike" to "probable hike," and that repricing moves markets significantly. Financials benefit. Gold falls. USD rallies. Growth stocks face additional pressure. Position for that scenario, not against it, going into his speech.

The futures picture tells a clear directional story for H2. Gold -14% in Q2 — the bear is structural. Oil -30% in Q2 — the supply glut is structural. Dollar +2% in Q2 — the bull is structural. These aren't short-term trades. They are multi-quarter trends driven by real macro forces (Fed hawkishness, Iran supply recovery, real rates rising) that are unlikely to reverse without a fundamental shift in those forces. Trade with these trends in H2. Short bounces in gold and oil. Buy dips in USD pairs.

NFP tomorrow (Thursday July 2) is the week's defining event. Given JOLTS at a two-year high, the upside risk to NFP is real — the labor market may be tighter than the 185K consensus forecast suggests. A print above 220K would send September hike probability above 90% and could trigger a significant cross-asset repricing: USD surging, bonds selling off, gold falling further, and growth stocks facing another leg lower. Size positions conservatively going into the print — holiday-thinned liquidity on Thursday and Friday amplifies moves in both directions.
