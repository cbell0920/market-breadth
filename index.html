The Inflation Peak Thesis

The most important macro question for equities over the next 4–6 weeks is whether May PCE at 4.1% represents the peak of the current inflation surge. Several analysts made this case Thursday:

The Iran war drove oil from ~$69 to ~$114/bbl, adding roughly 1.2 percentage points to PCE inflation between January and May 2026. The Strait of Hormuz has reopened and oil has fallen all the way back to $69/bbl. That 45-point decline in oil will feed through into June PCE (reported late July) and July PCE (reported August) as lower gasoline prices. If the disinflation shows up in June data, the Fed hike narrative softens materially and equities get a significant tailwind into Q3.

The risk to this thesis: services inflation (currently +2% m/m, +3.4% YoY on core) is driven by wages and shelter, not energy. Even if oil-driven inflation disappears, services inflation could keep core PCE sticky above 3%. That's the scenario where the Fed hikes in September and equities face a more difficult second half. The next 6 weeks of data will resolve this question.
